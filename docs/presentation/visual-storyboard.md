# Visual Storyboard

Last updated: June 13, 2026.

This is the design quality bar for the Gamma deck.

## Core Visual Idea

CrisisCoord should look like a calm crisis command room: focused, auditable, and operational. The deck should show coordination, not decoration. The best recurring visual motif is an incident room timeline where agent findings unlock the next action.

## Design System

Use a restrained interface palette:

- graphite for primary backgrounds
- off-white for readable surfaces
- alert red for risk
- amber for unresolved decisions
- trust blue for active coordination
- green only for contained or approved states

Typography should be sharp and modern. Use compact headings, short body copy, and diagram labels that survive a quick screen-share view.

## Slide Rhythm

Open with the incident, not the technology.

Move from crisis fragmentation to coordinated room state.

Make the Band dependency visible before naming every technical component.

Show partner usage as proof that the demo is real, not as the whole story.

End on accountability: speed matters, but the product wins because it preserves human control and traceability.

## Must-Have Visuals

The deck should include these proof objects:

- a crisis room timeline
- five agent lanes
- a dependency gate where Legal and Technical unlock Communications
- a human decision queue
- an audit ledger with source, timestamp, owner, and status
- a simple provisional architecture map

## Avoid

Do not use:

- spreadsheet screenshots
- dense tables
- fake metric cards
- giant percentages
- generic AI imagery
- stock-photo cyber drama
- decorative gradients without information
- architecture boxes that look final before the team has decided

## Hero Direction

The first card should say `CrisisCoord` clearly and make the product obvious in the first viewport. Use an interface-style hero: one incident, one room, visible agent lanes, and an unresolved decision state.

## Strongest Slide

The dependency gate should be the strongest slide in the deck.

The visual should make this obvious:

Legal finding + Technical finding -> Communications draft unlocked -> Human approval required.

That is the reason Band matters. Without that handoff, the product becomes a normal single-agent assistant, which is not enough for this hackathon.

## Final Pass Checklist

- Can a judge understand the product from the thumbnails alone?
- Does every slide have one claim?
- Is Band shown as the active coordination layer?
- Are AI/ML API and Featherless visible in the actual workflow?
- Are fake numbers removed?
- Are legal and compliance claims phrased as review support?
- Is the architecture explicitly provisional?
